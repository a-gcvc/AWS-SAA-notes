- You can't login to IAM groups and IAM groups have no credentials of their own.

- An IAM user can be a member of multiple IAM groups.

- Two benefits:
    - they allow effective, administration-style management of users
    - groups can actually have policies attached to them and this is both inline policies and managed policies


- **There isn't actually a bulit-in all-users group inside IAM so you don't have a single group that contains all of the members of that account like you do with some other identity management solutions.**

![alt text](<Screenshot 2025-08-17 124550.png>)

- You can't have groups inside groups.

- A policy on a resource can reference IAM users and IAM roles by using the ARN.

![alt text](<Screenshot 2025-08-17 124654.png>)

- A resource policy cannot grant access to an IAM group.
