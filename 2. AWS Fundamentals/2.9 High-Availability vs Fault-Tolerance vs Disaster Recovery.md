- **High Availability** (HA), **Fault-Tolerance** (FT) and **Disaster Recover** (DR) are three essential concepts to understand for every Solutions Architect.

![alt text](<Screenshot 2025-08-16 110555.png>)

- A HA system is one designed to be online and providing services as often as possible.

HA is not about user experience.

HA is about maximizing a system's online time and that's it.

![alt text](<Screenshot 2025-08-16 111214.png>)

- **Fault tolerance** means that if a system has faults, then it should continue to operate properly, even while faults are present and being fixed.

It means it has to continue operating through a failure without impacting customers.

Fault tolerance is harder to design, harder to implement and costs much more.

- **Disaster recovery** is about what to plan for and do when disaster occurs.

DR is designed to keep the crucial and non replaceable parts of your system safe, so when disaster ocurs, you don't lose anything irreplaceable and can rebuild after the disaster.
